How do I see the differences between HTML and CSS?

1. What is the difference between the usage of HTML and CSS?

HTML is used to create the actual content of the page, such as written text, and CSS is responsible for the design or style of the website, including the layout, visual effects and background color.

5. What is the difference between semantic and non-semantic elements?

Non-semantic elements in HTML don't give any idea other than their type. For example, < p > only gives us the idea of paragraph text. But semantic elements tell us what they are and how they relate to the webpage. For example, < header > indicates the place where the page begins and may contain some navigation links. < article > suggests a form, post, or newspaper article on the page.
